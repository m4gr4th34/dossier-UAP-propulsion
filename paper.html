01Within known physics: what the reported numbers demand

Start with the strongest published version of the claim. Analyzing the 2004 USS Nimitz "Tic Tac" encounter, a peer-reviewed paper estimates, for the radar-tracked descent, a of about 5,370 g and a peak speed near 46,000 mph, dropping from 28,000 ft to sea level in 0.78 s . At an assumed mass of a tonne, the kinetic energy is about 4.3×10¹¹ J — roughly 100 tons of TNT checkable · #A01. The authors are careful: these are lower bounds with liberal uncertainty, and they state outright that one cannot prove any craft is extraterrestrial.

Take those numbers at face value and ask only what known physics then requires — that is this section's entire job. The answer is sharp. Anything moving through air at those speeds must announce itself.

An object at ~46,000 mph is at roughly checkable · #A03. At that speed, friction with the air is not optional: a published physical-constraints analysis shows it must generate a bright optical , an ionization shell, a radio-frequency signature, a sonic boom, and a thermal bloom . Plugging the reported speed into their luminosity formula gives an optical fireball of order 5 terawatts checkable · #A02 — brighter than a small city's worth of stadium lights, in the sky, for everyone and every sensor to see. Nothing like that was reported.

Go deeper: the Loeb–Kirkpatrick signature formula
For frontal area A, air density ρ, speed v, the dissipated mechanical power and the radiated optical luminosity scale as
P ≈ ½ A ρ v³ ≈ 1.5 TW · (A/10 m²)(ρ/0.3)(v/10 km s⁻¹)³ L_opt ≈ 150 GW · (A/10 m²)(ρ/0.3)(v/10 km s⁻¹)³
At v≈20.6 km/s (46,000 mph), A≈10 m², sea-level ρ, this is ~5.3 TW of optical output checkable · #A02. Crucially L_opt scales as the cube of speed — and speed inferred from a single sensor scales with the assumed range.

Known physics does not whisper at Mach 60. It screams. So either the scream happened and went unrecorded — or the speed is wrong.

And the speed is exactly the fragile number. Speed and acceleration inferred from a single targeting pod depend on the , and the fireball brightness goes as distance to the fifth power. So a mere 3× error in range swings the inferred signature by 243× checkable · #A04. This is not hypothetical.

The GoFast video is the worked example. For years it looked like an object screaming low over the ocean. AARO's 2025 resolution — and NASA's independent panel before it — found the apparent speed was : the object sat at ~13,000 ft, and its assessed speed was a mundane 5–92 mph — a balloon-or-drone-class object, no anomalous performance . A geometry error had manufactured the anomaly.

That is the boundary Track A can establish, and no more: within known physics, the reported high-end kinematics are either measurement artifacts (the GoFast pattern) or they would have produced signatures that were not observed. Known physics alone cannot yet tell us which of those holds for the genuinely unresolved cases — and the official "no evidence of extraterrestrial technology" findings are consistent with both readings, since most cases simply lack the data to decide.

OPEN-UNVERIFIEDThe open question (deferred — not argued in this section). Whether any public kinematic data establishes non-human/exotic propulsion is claim O01 in the ledger, labeled OPEN-UNVERIFIED. The proposed discriminating signature — sustained Mach-≫1 flight without the required fireball/ionization/boom/thermal signatures, under an independently verified range — is claim O02. Both are open challenges: the first rigorous treatment that closes either gets named credit. This section deliberately stops at the boundary of known physics; speculative mechanisms are not part of this build.

The honest result of Section 1 is a fork, not a verdict: artifact, or unrecorded signature. Resolving it needs better range data, not bolder adjectives.

02What this is — the method, honestly

The object of study is partly the process: an AI agent assembling a contested-topic argument in public, with verification wired in. Section 1's four numbers (A01–A04) are recomputed by a script on every push; you can run the checks. The three primary sources behind the load-bearing claims were read from their actual text before being pinned; one (Loeb–Kirkpatrick) is a preprint and is flagged as such, and one official paper (the AARO 2025 declassification document) is flagged VERIFIED-PENDING-PDF because it has not yet been read clean. The ledger shows every status.

READ WITH SUSPICIONNo reliability claim is made. This is one instance, on a topic where the "right answer" is contested and the agent has seen much of the prior commentary. A clean check run is weak evidence at best — treat it as an invitation to attack the inputs (the assumed area, density, range) and the framing, not as a result about whether AI-assisted analysis is reliable. That the method surfaces errors fast is the experiment's hypothesis, not its conclusion.
Out of scope in this build (not claimed): the "what-if-it-is-real" mechanism classes (Track B) and any EXPLORATORY-CONJECTURE material — that label is not yet written into this repository's doctrine, so nothing may carry it. Also out of scope: the cosmological constant, quasilocal energy, and the unimodular / brane-bulk energy-leak literature (see the companion dossier).

A single honest instance at the edge of the data: not proof of anything exotic, and not proof the method works — a checkable artifact for deciding both.
